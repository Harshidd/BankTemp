# Reference Analysis: BoConcept Showroom Direction

## What Was Borrowed
- **Calm, Premium Spacing:** Lots of whitespace, highly disciplined padding, and a relaxed scroll rhythm.
- **Image Dominance:** The layout defers to imagery. Boxes and borders are minimized in favor of large, unconstrained photography.
- **Dual Flow:** The careful balance between exploring catalog/materials and exploring completed projects/interiors.
- **Typography:** Contrast between clean, modern sans-serif and elegant serif accents to denote luxury and craftsmanship.

## What Was Excluded
- **Hard E-Commerce Features:** No "Add to Cart" or complex product variation dropdowns. This is a showroom/studio template meant to generate leads, consultations, and showroom visits, not direct online sales.
- **Heavy Javascript Interactions:** Avoided complex scroll-jacking or heavy WebGL effects to keep the template fast, robust, and easily adaptable for standard WordPress/CMS setups later.
- **Aggressive Popups:** No newsletter modals or sticky discount bars.

## Local Adaptation (Turkey Market)
- **WhatsApp Prominence:** Added a clear "WhatsApp Consult" line in the contact section, which is critical for Turkish B2C and high-end B2B interactions.
- **Direct Address Formats:** Pre-formatted for standard Turkish addressing (e.g., Nisantasi).
- **Service Phasing:** Services are broken down explicitly (Discovery -> Concept -> Execution), which helps justify costs and build trust in the local market where turnkey project expectations are high.
